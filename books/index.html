The Best Polish Restaurant in Buffalo by William Kowalski

For adults and teens

A masterful blend of historical and modern fiction by a best-selling, award-winning author, The Best Polish Restaurant in Buffalo chronicles a century of life in America for one humble Polish farm girl and three generations of her descendants in Buffalo, NY.

For adults and teens

The Weight of Water by Sarah Crossan

For grades 6 - 8

Armed with a suitcase and an old laundry bag filled with clothes, Kasienka and her mother head for England. Life is lonely for both of them. When someone special swims into her life, Kasienka learns that there might be more than one way for her to stay afloat. The Weight of Water is a startlingly original piece of fiction: it also tackles the alienation experienced by many young immigrants.

Learn more about Sarah Crossan.

The Glass Mountain: Tales from Poland by David Walser, illustrated by Jan Pienkowski

For grades 3 - 5

Dragons and kings, frogs and spells, witches and mermaids; all the hallmark characters of traditional Polish fairy tales are found in this magical collection. Artist Pienkowski draws on a distinctive cut-paper technique learned as a child in Poland to produce dramatic and vibrant illustrations for eight time-honored stories.

Learn more about David Walser.

  • Skokie Public Library

Ignaś- The Boy with the Colorful Headby Anna Mycek-Wodecki

For grades 3 - 5

This delightful story will introduce children to the Polish language and a touch of Polish history about one of the country’s best known cultural assets. Mycek-Wodecki has written several children's books translated into seven languages: Minutka the Bilingual Dog series as well as How Would It Feel?, which she illustrated.

Learn more about Anna Mycek-Wodecki.
